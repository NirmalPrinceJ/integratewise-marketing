## KEY MESSAGE SHIFTS

### 1. From "Integration Problem" → "Ownership Problem"

**OLD**: "10 AIs × 100 systems = 1,000 integrations"
**NEW**: "AI Vendors Want to Own Your Infrastructure"

**Why**: The real problem isn't integration count — it's who owns:

- The integrations (vendor-locked)
- The memory (vendor-trapped)
- The governance (vendor-controlled)

### 2. From "Capability Fabric" → "Ownership Layer"

**OLD**: Enterprise AI Capability Fabric
**NEW**: Enterprise Ownership Layer for AI

**Why**: "Ownership" is what executives care about. "Capability Fabric" is technical jargon.

### 3. From "N+M Math" → "What You Own"

**OLD**: Focus on connection reduction (N×M → N+M)
**NEW**: Focus on ownership of three assets:

1. **Memory** — Your conversations, decisions, knowledge
2. **Governance** — Your authorization, audit trail, compliance
3. **Capabilities** — Your integrations, entity resolution, APIs

### 4. From "Rent Intelligence" → "Authorize Any Intelligence"

**OLD**: "Rent the Intelligence" (passive)
**NEW**: "Authorize Any Intelligence" (active, control-focused)

**Why**: Emphasizes you're in control, AI vendors need permission
