## 1. IntegrateWise in One View

IntegrateWise is building a new category of enterprise software: a **Knowledge Workspace empowered by AI and the Spine**.

It is designed for organizations that struggle with disconnected tools, fragmented knowledge, scattered workflows, weak operational visibility, and AI systems that are powerful but lack grounded business context.

At the heart of IntegrateWise is the **Adaptive Spine**—the unified intelligence layer and single source of truth that connects tools, data, conversations, signals, and decisions across the organization.

By organizing operational truth into the Spine, IntegrateWise creates a workspace where AI can understand context, reason across systems, and propose actions responsibly—while humans remain in control through approval-driven workflows.

The defining principle of IntegrateWise is simple:

**AI thinks in context — and waits for approval.**

That means intelligence is grounded in business truth, recommendations are evidence-linked, policy and permissions shape what is allowed, and meaningful actions do not execute until the right human approvals are in place.

IntegrateWise is not just a copilot, workflow automation layer, CRM overlay, knowledge base, or dashboard system. It is a new operating model for modern work—one that unifies truth, context, intelligence, and governed execution in a single environment.

## 7. What the Product Is

IntegrateWise is a workspace-first platform built in layers.

### Layer 0 — External Reality and Setup

This layer covers onboarding, tenancy, identity, connectors, source-system relationships, permissions setup, and external systems.

### Layer 1 — Workspace Layer

This is the visible user workspace. It contains the actual surfaces where people work: accounts, opportunities, tasks, meetings, documents, knowledge, metrics, operational records, and role-specific work environments.

Layer 1 must remain clean. It is a work surface, not a noisy AI screen.

### Layer 2 — Cognitive Intelligence Layer

This layer contains reasoning, evidence, recommendations, policy checks, action proposals, approvals, and governance interactions. It is where the system thinks, explains, and prepares action without forcing cognitive overlays into the workspace itself.

### Layer 3 — Platform and Services Layer

This layer contains ingestion, normalization, memory, orchestration, workflows, policy evaluation, audit logging, connector logic, sync orchestration, and execution services.

Together, these layers allow IntegrateWise to preserve clarity in work while delivering depth in intelligence and control in action.

## 11. The Product Operating Model

IntegrateWise runs on a governed intelligence cycle:

**Load → Normalize → Store → Think → Govern → Act → Repeat / Adjust**

### Load

Data and context enter the platform from connected systems, uploaded sources, event streams, messages, and knowledge inputs.

### Normalize

Incoming information is transformed into a structured and governed representation. Structured truth is mapped into the Spine. Contextual information is classified into the appropriate store. Evidence linkage is preserved.

### Store

Data is written to the correct system layer: canonical truth to the Spine, contextual material to context systems, and knowledge outputs to the relevant knowledge surfaces.

### Think

AI and intelligence services reason over the available truth, relationships, evidence, context, and policies to generate insights, summaries, recommendations, and action proposals.

### Govern

Policies, scopes, permissions, risk rules, and approval frameworks determine what may proceed, what must wait, and what requires review.

### Act

Approved actions can be executed through workflows, tool actions, messages, updates, or system changes.

### Repeat / Adjust

Outcomes are captured, denials are learned from, corrections are recorded, and the system improves its future proposals through governed feedback.

This is what allows IntegrateWise to be intelligent without becoming uncontrolled.

## 12. Flow Model

IntegrateWise organizes information across three foundational flows.

### Flow A — Structured Systems to Spine

Flow A handles structured business data from operational systems such as CRM, ticketing, finance, project, or workflow tools. This information is ingested, normalized, schema-bound, and stored in the Spine.

### Flow B — Unstructured Context to Context and Knowledge Layers

Flow B handles documents, notes, messages, content, and other contextual sources. These are linked as evidence and routed into the appropriate context or knowledge structures.

### Flow C — AI Sessions and Knowledge Outputs

Flow C handles AI conversations, summaries, generated outputs, and reasoning artifacts. These support understanding, collaboration, and knowledge work. They do not become canonical truth automatically.

The purpose of this three-flow model is to preserve integrity.
Structured truth, contextual evidence, and generated knowledge must each have a valid place in the system without being confused with one another.

## 14. Human-in-the-Loop and Approval-First Execution

IntegrateWise is built on an approval-first philosophy.

This means the system may think, recommend, summarize, score, prioritize, and prepare actions—but it does not assume that execution should happen without review when risk, impact, or policy requires human control.

Approval-first execution includes:

* policy-aware proposal creation,
* scoped permissions,
* human review paths,
* approve / reject / edit decisions,
* audit logging,
* risk-based controls,
* learning from rejection or correction.

This design is central to the product identity.

It is how IntegrateWise avoids the common trap of making AI feel powerful at the cost of enterprise trust.
